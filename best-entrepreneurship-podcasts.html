The Best Business Podcasts for SMB Owners

Podcasts have become one of the highest-value business education channels for entrepreneurs — the format is perfect for a business owner who can listen during a commute, on a job site, or between calls. The best entrepreneurship podcasts combine tactical specificity with genuine practitioner experience, covering real numbers and real challenges rather than vague inspiration.

My First Million (Shaan Puri & Sam Parr) is one of the most popular and most recommended business podcasts in the SMB community. The format — two entrepreneurs riffing on business ideas, industry opportunities, and tactical growth strategies — is both entertaining and surprisingly educational. Episodes regularly surface underserved niches, acquisition opportunities, and marketing playbooks that listeners put to work immediately.

The Home Service Expert (Tommy Mello) is the definitive podcast for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, lawn care, cleaning, and general field service business owners. Tommy interviews successful home service operators weekly, covering the specific metrics, software, pricing, and hiring strategies that move the needle for service businesses. If you run any kind of home service company, this is required listening.

How I Built This (Guy Raz / NPR) covers the founding stories of iconic companies — from Airbnb and Spanx to Warby Parker and SoulCycle. It's less tactical and more inspirational, but the pattern recognition from 400+ episodes of founder stories is genuinely valuable for anyone building a business.

The GaryVee Audio Experience covers marketing, brand building, and entrepreneurship from Gary Vaynerchuk's perspective. Best for: mindset, social media strategy, and long-term brand thinking. Gary's content is high-volume and energetic — best consumed selectively rather than end-to-end.

Contrarian Thinking (Codie Sanchez) covers business acquisition, cash flow investing, and building income through "boring businesses." The podcast and accompanying newsletter are particularly valuable for entrepreneurs interested in buying existing businesses as a wealth-building strategy.

Best Podcasts by Business Type

For Home Service Business Owners: The Home Service Expert (Tommy Mello) is the clear #1. Supplemental listens include the HVAC School Podcast (Bryan Orr) for HVAC businesses, and the Contractor Fight (Tom Reber) for general contractors and remodelers. These podcasts understand the specific math and operations of field service businesses in ways general entrepreneurship shows don't.

For E-Commerce Operators: My Wife Quit Her Job (Steve Chou) covers e-commerce from a practical, profit-focused perspective. The Unofficial Shopify Podcast covers platform-specific tactics. 2X eCommerce (Kunle Campbell) focuses on scaling Shopify brands through data-driven marketing. For Amazon FBA specifically, Helium 10's Serious Sellers Podcast is one of the most information-dense resources available.

For Franchise Buyers and Operators: Franchise Secrets (Erik Van Horn) is the most transparent and practitioner-driven franchise podcast available. Joel Libava's content (the Franchise King) offers complementary, buyer-focused education. Both cover due diligence, FDD analysis, multi-unit operations, and the realities of franchisee life with candor.

For Sales Professionals and Business Owners: Make It Happen Mondays (John Barrows) covers B2B sales tactics weekly. Sell or Die (Jeffrey Gitomer and Jennifer Glick) covers sales mindset and technique. The Advanced Selling Podcast (Bill Caskey and Bryan Neale) is one of the longest-running sales podcasts with practical, non-hype advice for salespeople at all levels.

For Productivity and Leadership: Deep Questions (Cal Newport) covers deep work, digital minimalism, and focused productivity in a Q&A format. Beyond the To-Do List (Erik Fisher) covers productivity systems and habits. The Michael Hyatt Podcast covers leadership, planning, and building a business that doesn't run you.

How to Get the Most From Business Podcasts

The most common mistake podcast listeners make is treating business content as passive entertainment. You learn from a podcast when you stop it, take a note, and implement something — not when you listen to it while distracted. The highest-ROI approach: keep a dedicated note (in Notion, Apple Notes, or a physical notebook) called "Podcast Action Items" and add one specific, implementable takeaway from each episode before moving on.

Volume is not the goal. Many entrepreneurs listen to 5–10 hours of podcast content per week and implement nothing. One hour of targeted, implementable content is worth more than ten hours of passive listening. Pick 2–3 shows that match your current biggest business challenge and go deep — listen to back episodes, implement their frameworks, and only add new shows when you've genuinely absorbed and applied what you're already consuming.

Use podcast content to identify the educators you want to go deeper with — then go to their YouTube channel, buy their book, or join their community. Podcasts are excellent for breadth and pattern recognition; books, courses, and communities are better for depth.
