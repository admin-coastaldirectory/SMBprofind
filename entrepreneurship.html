📚 Must-Read Books

📗
Profit First
Mike Michalowicz's system for transforming any business from a cash-eating monster to a money-making machine. The #1 book for small business owners.
Book
📘
Buy Then Build
Walker Deibel's definitive guide to entrepreneurship through acquisition — skip the startup phase and buy an existing profitable business.
Book
📗
The E-Myth Revisited
Michael Gerber's seminal book on why most small businesses fail — and how to fix it. The core insight: business owners must work on the business, not just in it. Introduces the Franchise Prototype model and the three personalities every owner must manage: entrepreneur, manager, and technician.
Book
📕
$100M Offers (Alex Hormozi)
Alex Hormozi's framework for creating offers so good people feel stupid saying no. Covers value equation, pricing, bonuses, guarantees, and naming — teaching any business owner how to charge premium prices and eliminate price resistance. One of the most-shared business books of the last 5 years.
Book
📘
Company of One (Paul Jarvis)
A counter-narrative to growth-at-all-costs — Paul Jarvis argues that staying lean, independent, and focused can lead to more profit, freedom, and sustainability than constant expansion. Must-read for solopreneurs and lifestyle business owners who want to optimize for margin, not headcount.
Book
📕
$100M Leads (Alex Hormozi)
The follow-up to $100M Offers — focuses specifically on lead generation. Hormozi breaks down every major traffic channel (content, cold outreach, paid ads, affiliates) with his 'Core Four' framework. Practical playbooks for getting more customers across any business model.
Book
⏰
Clockwork (Mike Michalowicz)
Michalowicz's system for designing a business that runs itself. Teaches owners to identify and delegate the Queen Bee Role, systematize operations, and eventually take a 4-week vacation without the business breaking. Critical reading for service business owners stuck working in the business.
Book
📊
Simple Numbers (Greg Crabtree)
Greg Crabtree's no-nonsense guide to using financial data to grow a profitable small business. Introduces 'simple numbers' benchmarks for labor efficiency, profit targets, and capital management that every owner-operator should track. Written for business owners, not accountants.
Book
🎯
Never Split the Difference
Former FBI hostage negotiator Chris Voss's negotiation playbook — tactical empathy, mirroring, calibrated questions, and the accusation audit. Immediately applicable to sales calls, vendor negotiations, lease renewals, and employee conversations. One of the most practically useful books for any business owner.
Book
🧠
Deep Work (Cal Newport)
Cal Newport argues that focused, distraction-free work is the most valuable skill in the modern economy — and increasingly rare. Provides rules and rituals for producing your best work in less time. Essential for entrepreneurs who are constantly reactive and never in flow state.
Book
📗
They Ask You Answer (Marcus Sheridan)
Marcus Sheridan's content marketing bible — born from saving his pool company during the 2008 recession by answering customer questions honestly online. The framework for using radical transparency in content to become the most trusted source in your industry. Practical for any local or service business.
Book
🚚
The Successful Owner-Operator
A practical guide specifically for trucking owner-operators — covering freight rates, fuel management, compliance, load boards, and building a carrier authority. Written by an industry veteran for the small fleet operator trying to move from driving to running a business.
Book
📖
Predictable Revenue (Book)
Aaron Ross and Marylou Tyler's foundational book on outbound sales systems, including the Cold Calling 2.0 methodology that generated $100M in ARR at Salesforce. Required reading for anyone building a B2B sales team.
Book
📖
Sell or Be Sold (Book)
Grant Cardone's foundational book on the sales mindset — arguing that everything in life is a sale and that learning to sell is the most important skill any entrepreneur or salesperson can develop.
Book
📖
The Little Red Book of Selling (Book)
Jeffrey Gitomer's 12.5 Principles of Sales Greatness — a pocket-sized masterclass on customer loyalty, referral generation, and why people buy. One of the bestselling sales books of all time for consumer-facing reps.
Book
📖
The Sales Development Playbook (Book)
Trish Bertuzzi's definitive guide to building a high-performing SDR team for SaaS companies. Covers hiring profiles, compensation structures, metrics, sequencing strategy, and how to align sales development with marketing.
