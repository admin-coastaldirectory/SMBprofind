Free Small Business Resources in Wisconsin

Wisconsin small business owners have access to a wide range of free and low-cost support programs backed by the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA). Whether you're launching a new venture or growing an existing business, these resources provide expert guidance, financing assistance, and training — all at no cost.

Small Business Development Centers (SBDC) in Wisconsin

The Wisconsin SBDC network provides free one-on-one business counseling and low-cost training to entrepreneurs across the state. Funded by the SBA and state government, SBDC advisors can help you write a business plan, apply for financing, analyze your market, and grow your operation.

SCORE Mentors in Wisconsin

SCORE connects Wisconsin entrepreneurs with experienced volunteer business mentors who provide free, confidential advice. With chapters across Wisconsin, SCORE offers both in-person and virtual mentoring sessions at no charge.

Women's Business Centers in Wisconsin

Women's Business Centers (WBCs) in Wisconsin provide specialized support for women entrepreneurs, including access to capital, training, and networking opportunities. These SBA-funded centers serve women at all stages of business ownership.

Veterans Business Resources in Wisconsin

Veterans starting or growing businesses in Wisconsin can access specialized counseling through Veterans Business Outreach Centers (VBOCs), which provide business plan development, feasibility analysis, and government contracting assistance.

All resources listed are free or low-cost. Contact each organization directly to schedule an appointment or attend a workshop.
