Free Small Business Resources in Idaho — Complete 2025 Guide

By SMBProFind Team · Updated 2025 · 8 min read

Idaho small business owners have access to a comprehensive network of free and low-cost support programs — from SBA-backed SBDC centers and SCORE mentors to state economic development agencies and local chambers of commerce. This guide covers every major resource available to Idaho entrepreneurs in 2025.

1. Idaho SBDC Network

The Idaho SBDC, hosted by Boise State University, provides free one-on-one business counseling and low-cost training to entrepreneurs across Idaho. SBDC advisors help with business planning, financial analysis, loan preparation, market research, export assistance, and government contracting — all at no charge.

Find your nearest Idaho SBDC center and schedule a free consultation at idahosbdc.org/

💡 First-time SBDC tip: Come to your first appointment with basic financial information — monthly revenue, expenses, and what you specifically need help with. The more specific your question, the more valuable the session will be.

2. SCORE Idaho Chapters

SCORE connects Idaho entrepreneurs with experienced volunteer business mentors — free, confidential, and available in-person or virtually. Idaho has several active SCORE chapters:

  • SCORE Boise
  • SCORE Eastern Idaho
  • SCORE Twin Falls

Find a Idaho SCORE mentor and schedule a free session at score.org

3. Women's Business Centers in Idaho

Idaho has Women's Business Centers providing specialized support for women entrepreneurs, including access to capital, business training, and networking. Key resources include Idaho WBC and local SBA-funded WBCs across the state.

Find Women's Business Centers near you at sba.gov

4. Veterans Business Resources in Idaho

  • Veterans Business Outreach Center (VBOC) — Free counseling, business planning, and government contracting guidance for veteran entrepreneurs
  • Boots to Business — SBA entrepreneurship training program available at Idaho military installations
  • Idaho Department of Veterans Affairs — State-level veteran business programs and resources
  • SBA Veteran-Owned Small Business Programs — Set-aside contracts and certification programs

5. Idaho State Programs

Idaho Department of Commerce is Idaho's primary economic development agency, offering a range of programs for small businesses:

  • Idaho Opportunity Fund
  • Workforce Development Training Fund
  • Rural Community Block Grants
  • Idaho Housing and Finance Association loans

Learn more at commerce.idaho.gov/

6. Local Economic Development Resources

  • Boise Valley Economic Partnership (BVEP) — tech and manufacturing site selection
  • Idaho Falls EDC — energy sector and rural business
  • Twin Falls EDC — agriculture and food processing
  • Idaho SBDC at BSU — startup and rural business counseling
  • Idaho CDFI — Community Development Financial Institution providing microloans and business coaching for underserved entrepreneurs
  • Boise Valley Economic Partnership — Local economic development organization offering business incentives and site selection assistance

7. Idaho Key Industries

Idaho's economy is anchored by Agriculture, technology, food processing, outdoor recreation, and manufacturing. Your local SBDC advisors are familiar with these sectors and can provide industry-specific guidance on financing, licensing, market analysis, and growth strategies.

🔗 Use all resources together: The most successful Idaho entrepreneurs don't use just one resource — they combine SBDC counseling for specific projects, SCORE mentors for ongoing guidance, and state programs for financing incentives. All three are free or low-cost.
