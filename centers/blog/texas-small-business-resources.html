📍 Texas

Free Small Business Resources in Texas — Complete 2025 Guide

By SMBProFind Team · Updated 2025 · 10 min read

Texas is one of the most business-friendly states in the country, and that extends to its network of free small business resources. With four separate SBDC regional networks, multiple SCORE chapters in every major metro, and a strong CDFI ecosystem serving underserved entrepreneurs, Texas business owners have exceptional access to free help.

1. Texas SBDC Networks

Texas is unique in having four separate regional SBDC networks, each covering a different part of the state. All are SBA-funded and offer free consulting.

  • Texas SBDC at UT Austin — Central and West Texas
  • Texas A&M SBDC Network — East Texas, Gulf Coast, and Brazos Valley
  • UTSA SBDC — South Texas and San Antonio metro
  • North Texas SBDC at UNT Dallas — DFW metroplex

All four networks share the txsbdc.org portal where you can find your nearest center and schedule a free consultation.

💡 Texas SBDC networks have strong government contracting programs — if you want to sell to federal, state, or local government agencies, your SBDC can help you get certified and find opportunities.

2. SCORE Texas Chapters

  • SCORE Houston — Energy, healthcare, and manufacturing expertise
  • SCORE Dallas — Finance, tech, and retail focus
  • SCORE San Antonio — Military, healthcare, and tourism industries
  • SCORE Austin — Technology startups and creative industries

3. Women's Business Centers in Texas

  • NAWBO Houston — Networking and resources for women-owned businesses in Houston
  • Women's Business Council Southwest — Dallas-based WBC with certification and contracting programs
  • LiftFund Women's Programs — San Antonio-based microloans specifically for women entrepreneurs

4. Veterans Business Resources

  • VBOC at McLennan Community College — Waco — serving Central and South Texas veterans
  • Boots to Business — SBA program available at all Texas military bases for transitioning service members
  • Texas Veterans Commission — State veterans benefits including business assistance grants

5. CDFIs & Microloans in Texas

Texas has some of the strongest CDFI lenders in the country, particularly serving Hispanic and minority entrepreneurs:

  • LiftFund — $500 to $1M loans, serving 13 states but headquartered in San Antonio
  • PeopleFund — Austin-based, $500 to $350K loans, strong women and minority focus
  • BCL of Texas — Business loans + homeownership, statewide reach
  • Texas Mezzanine Fund — Larger growth capital for Texas small businesses

6. State Programs

  • Texas Enterprise Fund — Job creation grants for relocating or expanding businesses
  • Texas Governor's Small Business Program — State-level resources and referrals
  • Texas Economic Development & Tourism — Export assistance, market development
  • Texas Workforce Commission — Free workforce training grants for small businesses

7. Metro-Specific Resources

Houston

  • Greater Houston Partnership — business development and networking
  • Houston Business Development Inc — minority small business loans
  • SBA Houston District Office — SBA loans and certifications

Dallas-Fort Worth

  • Dallas Economic Development — business incentives and site selection
  • Fort Worth Economic Development — small business programs and incentives
  • SBDC at UNT Dallas — free DFW counseling

Austin

  • Austin Chamber of Commerce — tech startup ecosystem and networking
  • City of Austin Small Business Program — local incentives and licensing
  • Austin Technology Incubator — for tech startups

San Antonio

  • San Antonio Economic Development Foundation — site selection and incentives
  • UTSA SBDC — free counseling for SA entrepreneurs
  • LiftFund HQ — microloans and business coaching
