Free Small Business Resources in Alaska — Complete 2025 Guide

By SMBProFind Team · Updated 2025 · 8 min read

Alaska small business owners have access to a comprehensive network of free and low-cost support programs — from SBA-backed SBDC centers and SCORE mentors to state economic development agencies and local chambers of commerce. This guide covers every major resource available to Alaska entrepreneurs in 2025.

1. Alaska SBDC Network

The Alaska SBDC, hosted by University of Alaska Anchorage, provides free one-on-one business counseling and low-cost training to entrepreneurs across Alaska. SBDC advisors help with business planning, financial analysis, loan preparation, market research, export assistance, and government contracting — all at no charge.

Find your nearest Alaska SBDC center and schedule a free consultation at aksbdc.org/

💡 First-time SBDC tip: Come to your first appointment with basic financial information — monthly revenue, expenses, and what you specifically need help with. The more specific your question, the more valuable the session will be.

2. SCORE Alaska Chapters

SCORE connects Alaska entrepreneurs with experienced volunteer business mentors — free, confidential, and available in-person or virtually. Alaska has several active SCORE chapters:

  • SCORE Anchorage
  • SCORE Fairbanks

Find a Alaska SCORE mentor and schedule a free session at score.org

3. Women's Business Centers in Alaska

Alaska has Women's Business Centers providing specialized support for women entrepreneurs, including access to capital, business training, and networking. Key resources include Alaska WBC and local SBA-funded WBCs across the state.

Find Women's Business Centers near you at sba.gov

4. Veterans Business Resources in Alaska

  • Veterans Business Outreach Center (VBOC) — Free counseling, business planning, and government contracting guidance for veteran entrepreneurs
  • Boots to Business — SBA entrepreneurship training program available at Alaska military installations
  • Alaska Department of Veterans Affairs — State-level veteran business programs and resources
  • SBA Veteran-Owned Small Business Programs — Set-aside contracts and certification programs

5. Alaska State Programs

Alaska Division of Economic Development is Alaska's primary economic development agency, offering a range of programs for small businesses:

  • Alaska Industrial Development Authority (AIDEA)
  • Alaska Small Business Development Center
  • Microenterprise Development programs

Learn more at www.commerce.alaska.gov/web/ded/

6. Local Economic Development Resources

  • Anchorage Economic Development Corp — business development and Arctic resources
  • Fairbanks Economic Development Corp — interior Alaska and mining sector
  • Alaska SBDC at UAA — export and rural business specialty
  • Alaska CDFI Network — Community Development Financial Institution providing microloans and business coaching for underserved entrepreneurs
  • AIDEA Alaska — Local economic development organization offering business incentives and site selection assistance

7. Alaska Key Industries

Alaska's economy is anchored by Tourism, oil and gas, fishing, transportation, and Arctic resources. Your local SBDC advisors are familiar with these sectors and can provide industry-specific guidance on financing, licensing, market analysis, and growth strategies.

🔗 Use all resources together: The most successful Alaska entrepreneurs don't use just one resource — they combine SBDC counseling for specific projects, SCORE mentors for ongoing guidance, and state programs for financing incentives. All three are free or low-cost.
