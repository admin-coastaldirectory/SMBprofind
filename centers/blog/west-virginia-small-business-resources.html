Free Small Business Resources in West Virginia — Complete 2025 Guide

By SMBProFind Team · Updated 2025 · 8 min read

West Virginia small business owners have access to a comprehensive network of free and low-cost support programs — from SBA-backed SBDC centers and SCORE mentors to state economic development agencies and local chambers of commerce. This guide covers every major resource available to West Virginia entrepreneurs in 2025.

1. West Virginia SBDC Network

The West Virginia SBDC, hosted by West Virginia Development Office, provides free one-on-one business counseling and low-cost training to entrepreneurs across West Virginia. SBDC advisors help with business planning, financial analysis, loan preparation, market research, export assistance, and government contracting — all at no charge.

Find your nearest West Virginia SBDC center and schedule a free consultation at wvsbdc.com/

💡 First-time SBDC tip: Come to your first appointment with basic financial information — monthly revenue, expenses, and what you specifically need help with. The more specific your question, the more valuable the session will be.

2. SCORE West Virginia Chapters

SCORE connects West Virginia entrepreneurs with experienced volunteer business mentors — free, confidential, and available in-person or virtually. West Virginia has several active SCORE chapters:

  • SCORE West Virginia
  • SCORE Charleston
  • SCORE Morgantown
  • SCORE Huntington

Find a West Virginia SCORE mentor and schedule a free session at score.org

3. Women's Business Centers in West Virginia

West Virginia has Women's Business Centers providing specialized support for women entrepreneurs, including access to capital, business training, and networking. Key resources include West Virginia WBC and local SBA-funded WBCs across the state.

Find Women's Business Centers near you at sba.gov

4. Veterans Business Resources in West Virginia

  • Veterans Business Outreach Center (VBOC) — Free counseling, business planning, and government contracting guidance for veteran entrepreneurs
  • Boots to Business — SBA entrepreneurship training program available at West Virginia military installations
  • West Virginia Department of Veterans Affairs — State-level veteran business programs and resources
  • SBA Veteran-Owned Small Business Programs — Set-aside contracts and certification programs

5. West Virginia State Programs

West Virginia Department of Economic Development is West Virginia's primary economic development agency, offering a range of programs for small businesses:

  • WV Economic Development Authority (WVEDA) loans
  • Appalachian Regional Commission (ARC) grants
  • Manufacturing Investment Tax Credit
  • Coalfield Economic Development grants

Learn more at wvded.wv.gov/

6. Local Economic Development Resources

  • Charleston Area Alliance — energy and healthcare sector
  • Morgantown Mon EDC — university partnerships and tech sector
  • WVEDA — statewide business loans and bond financing
  • Coalfield Development Corp — rural economic development and social enterprise
  • Coalfield Development Corp — Community Development Financial Institution providing microloans and business coaching for underserved entrepreneurs
  • Charleston Area Alliance — Local economic development organization offering business incentives and site selection assistance

7. West Virginia Key Industries

West Virginia's economy is anchored by Energy (coal and natural gas transitioning to renewables), healthcare, manufacturing, and forestry. Your local SBDC advisors are familiar with these sectors and can provide industry-specific guidance on financing, licensing, market analysis, and growth strategies.

🔗 Use all resources together: The most successful West Virginia entrepreneurs don't use just one resource — they combine SBDC counseling for specific projects, SCORE mentors for ongoing guidance, and state programs for financing incentives. All three are free or low-cost.
